Here's the part we gate behind the demo paywall — the honest launch checklist we used for Morse itself:

1. **Fix the funnel before the marketing.** Every quick start must work verbatim, first try, for a stranger. One dead link costs more trust than ten features buy.
2. **Tell users what breaks.** Testnet wipes, epoch expiry, unaudited contracts — early adopters forgive everything they were warned about and nothing they weren't.
3. **Make the demo clickable.** Nobody funds a wallet to evaluate a CMS. This blog exists so you can judge Morse without touching a faucet.
4. **Let agents in the door.** Machine-readable docs and a scriptable CLI mean your earliest "users" might not be human — and they file surprisingly good bug reports.
